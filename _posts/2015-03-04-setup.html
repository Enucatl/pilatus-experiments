The source is a Comet MXR 160/HP-11 tube operated at 160 kVp and 10 mA. Used for grating interferometry with 120 keV, 100 keV and 60 keV design energy. The experiments presented today were realized with a 120 keV, 3rd Talbot order setup.

The gratings need to be very thick to block the radiation with an energy up to 160 keV, and a small period is necessary to achieve high sensitivity of the interferometer. This makes a 2D grating not feasible with the current microfabrication technology. Therefore, we illuminate the gratings from the edge, so that the light travels through the long side of the grating. A high aspect ratio is achieved at the cost of the vertical spatial dimension.
